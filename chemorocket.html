Chem-o-rocket

Chem-o-Rocket is a fun event with an element of chemistry in it aiming at running a model rocket on chemical reactions. Participants will have to make their own rocket model as per their design and skills. The event is structured as a competition, with teams competing to see whose rocket can reach the highest altitude or complete a specific task, such as carrying a payload or landing in a designated target area. The technical aspects of the event would challenge participants to apply their knowledge of chemical engineering to create a successful rocket design, while also promoting teamwork and collaboration among participants.

Event Description

Here are the steps to make a working model of a Chem-O-rocket:

Materials Needed:
• Two-liter plastic bottle
• Cardboard sheets
• Scissors
• Glue
• Duct tape
• Compressed air pump
• Launch pad and stand

Instructions:
1. Cut two fins from the cardboard sheets and attach them to the bottom of the rocket with glue.
2. Cut a circular opening in the cardboard sheet that is slightly smaller than the diameter of the bottle neck.
3. Insert the bottle into the opening and secure it with duct tape.
4. Build a launch pad and stand by attaching a piece of wood or PVC pipe to a base plate.
5. Attach the compressed air pump to the launch pad using a hose.
6. Place the Chen-O rocket onto the launch pad and connect the hose from the pump to the bottle.
7. Pump air into the bottle until it reaches the desired pressure level.
8. Stand back and push the launch button to release the rocket into the air.
9. Recover the rocket using a parachute or other recovery system.
10. Repeat steps 6-9 for multiple launches.

Note: Always follow the safety guidelines and regulations for water rocket launches, and avoid launching rockets in areas where they could cause harm to people or property

Event Rules

1.Safety first: Ensure that all participants and spectators understand the safety guidelines and follow them at all times.
2.Design specifications: Rockets should be made of lightweight materials such as plastic or cardboard and must not exceed a certain weight or size limit. Pressure limits: Rockets must not be pressurized beyond a certain limit, which will be specified by the event organizers.

General Rules

1. Launch area: Rockets should be launched from a designated area that is clear of any obstructions, including people and buildings.
2.No interference: Participants and spectators should not interfere with other rockets during launch or flight.
3.Flight path: Rockets should be designed to fly in a straight trajectory and must not pose any danger to people or property.
4.Recovery: Rockets must be designed with a recovery system such as a parachute to ensure safe landing and retrieval.
5.Time limits: Each rocket should only be launched within a specific time limit, which will be determined by the event organizers.
6.Fair play: Participants should compete in the spirit of fair play and sportsmanship, and any cheating or unsportsmanlike conduct will not be tolerated.
7.Environmental responsibility: Participants should ensure that their rockets are recovered and disposed of responsibly after the event.
